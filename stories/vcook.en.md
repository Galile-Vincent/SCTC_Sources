# The Story of Building VCook

![img 001](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ea6b4d52-8c08-4459-b032-d669125beb86)

I built VCook because of my mom.

Every day after work, she was already exhausted, yet she still had to think about one more thing: **what to cook tonight**.  
And once she started cooking, there were often even more problems waiting for her.

Seeing that made me realize that cooking is not just about following a recipe.  
It is actually a process full of small decisions, friction, and stress.

So I started breaking the cooking journey down and analyzing where the real problems happen.  
I found that cooking can roughly be divided into three main stages:

1. Finding a recipe  
2. Buying ingredients  
3. Cooking the meal  

And in each of these stages, there are pain points that people deal with all the time.

## 1. Finding a recipe

Sometimes the hardest part is not cooking itself, but simply deciding **what to cook**.

For many busy people, especially after a long day, thinking of meal ideas is already mentally tiring.  
On top of that, many recipe platforms are not very friendly to people with specific dietary needs or health goals.

That is why, in VCook, I wanted users to see recommended recipes as soon as they open the app.  
Not just random recommendations, but suggestions that can better match different needs, such as fitness goals, health information, or personal dietary preferences.

## 2. Buying ingredients

Another common problem happens before cooking even begins.

You find a recipe you want to make, but when you go shopping, one of the ingredients is sold out or unavailable.  
This happens all the time, and it often breaks the whole plan.

So I designed VCook to help with this too.  
With a simple swipe, users can find ingredient substitutions that still fit the dish and make sense for the recipe, so cooking does not stop just because one item is missing.

## 3. Cooking the meal

Even though many recipe apps already have step-by-step guidance, there is still a very practical issue in the kitchen:

**your hands get dirty, and you cannot keep touching the screen.**

That is something I felt many products did not truly consider from a real cooking perspective.

So VCook includes gesture control.  
Instead of tapping the screen while cooking, users can simply wave their hand in front of the device to move through the recipe steps.  
It makes the experience much more natural and better suited for the kitchen.

## How I built VCook

I built the first working version of VCook in just **three weeks** from idea to app.

But after putting it out and receiving feedback, one thing became very clear:  
the interface design was still not strong enough.

That also made me realize one of the biggest limitations of AI right now.  
AI can help you build features very quickly, but it does not always create a polished or thoughtful interface on the first try.

Because of that, I spent a lot of time iterating on the UI together with AI.

The most effective workflow I found was:

- showing AI screenshots of interfaces I liked and asking it to build in that direction
- taking screenshots of parts it got wrong and telling it exactly what needed to be fixed

To me, this process felt less like “AI builds everything for you” and more like **working with AI as a fast but imperfect design partner**.

## What VCook means to me

To me, VCook is not just a recipe app.

It is my attempt to solve a very real and very human everyday problem:
when someone is already tired, can technology make cooking feel a little easier?

That simple question was where VCook began.

I wanted to build something that could reduce stress, support real-life cooking situations, and help people feel a little more cared for in their daily lives.

And that is what VCook means to me.
